Featured Poem

Mortis mihi: My Death

I’m in a pool of remorse, where I drown everyday.

I’m abject, numb and a scum.

Admonishing my failures has become a coping mechanism.

I strangle myself everyday, suffocating the sense of life within.

Told myself lies for the leverage.

Hold grudges against myself for living in a cage.

Deluded myself for a sense of subdue.

I despise myself, condemn myself.

Embraced my demise as the loved one.

Disgraced my liberty as it’s the cold one.

The course of self-loathing is insatiable.

Unaware of egregious lesions, aware of incomprehensible conclusions.

My soul is rusted and my sufferings become nascent.

Dissonance

I feel a sense of sadness blooming deep within me.

Giving birth to flowers of ordeal dismay.

Lingering in my soul, making it cold.

Numbing the essence of life.

Creating a vortex, making everything incongruous.

Inciting hollowness, claiming its leverage.

There are no amends, nor can there ever be.

There is no end, nor there shall be.

There is dissonance, all there will be.
